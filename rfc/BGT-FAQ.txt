This document is a response cache, not an enumeration.  Infinite
surface variations of "why Bitcoin bad" reduce to six generative
Core Value Conflicts (VC1-VC6).  Any question not matching an
existing entry routes through the same VC to the formal defense
document.  The FAQ grows only when a new sub-topic requires a
distinct atomic answer.

1. CLASSIFY: Route user's surface language through [BGT-0000]
   Objection Routing to identify the Core Value Conflict (VC1-VC6).

2. LOOKUP: Find the FAQ entry matching that VC and sub-topic
   (see VC Index below).

3. RESPOND: Use the Atomic Answer as the immediate opening hook.

4. EXPAND: Use the Ref to pull axiomatic proofs from RFC documents.

5. TONE: Neutral, structural -- frame as "Systemic Necessity" not
   "Good Investment."

Framework questions (AI/AGI, game theory proofs) are not objections.
They do not map to VCs and route directly to Sections 9-10.

SOVEREIGNTY QUESTIONS
================================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q: Won't they ban it?  [VC2]
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ATOMIC ANSWER:
Suppression in one jurisdiction triggers emergence in others (Whack-a-Mole)
due to global energy arbitrage.

REFS:
- [BGT-0001] T1 (Suppression/Arbitrage dynamic)
- [BGT-0005] A2.1 (Global Ban Defense)

PREDATOR SELF-INTEREST (Gridlock Wedge):
Beyond whack-a-mole, enforcement actors (coordination taxers CT1-CT3)
actively preserve the neutral rail to hedge against rival enforcers'
monetary weaponization.  Banning Bitcoin removes a sovereign's hedge
against dollar weaponization, SWIFT exclusion, or rival sanctions.
Each enforcer's dominant strategy is to preserve access ([BGT-0001]
S10, G1-G6; [BGT-0002] Qg5).

EVIDENCE:
- China's 2021 ban: hashrate recovered to ATH within 6 months [BGT-0008]
  Entry 2.4.2
- Singapore issued record 13 DPT licenses in 2024 [BGT-0008] Entry 2.1.2
- Russia/Iran use Bitcoin for sanctions evasion while nominally
  restricting domestic use—dual-role behavior (CT1 + EV2)

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q: Why would a dictator want it?  [VC2]
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ATOMIC ANSWER:
It prevents middle-management corruption by providing a "corruption-proof"
audit trail that subordinates cannot alter.

REFS:
- [BGT-0001] A3 (Dictator's Audit Problem)
- [BGT-0005] A2.2 (Dictator's Audit Defense)

KEY INSIGHT:
Autocrats face the same principal-agent problems as democracies.  Bitcoin
solves theft-by-subordinate better than any surveillance system.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q: Why must governments print money?  [VC2]
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ATOMIC ANSWER:
Politicians debase to fund promises.  They avoid political suicide.
It is a survival strategy.

REFS:
- [BGT-0001] E1 (Self-Reinforcing Exit Advantage - debasement creates exit pressure)
- [BGT-0005] A2.3 (Fiat Debase Defense)

EVIDENCE:
- No nation has repaid debt >120% GDP through taxation alone [BGT-0008] Entry
  2.1.1
- Global debt-to-GDP exceeded 300% in 2024 [BGT-0008] Entry 2.1.1

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q: Can they seize it?  [VC2]
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ATOMIC ANSWER:
Bitcoin's security is informational, not physical.  There is no vault to raid.
Seizure requires coordinating coercion across distributed keyholders—a
categorically harder problem.

REFS:
- [BGT-0001] P6 (Brain Keys / Informational Security)
- [BGT-0005] A2.4 (Key Seizure Defense)

KEY INSIGHT:
Attack surface shifts from "raid the vault" to "coordinate global coercion."
The latter is categorically harder, especially for cross-border holdings.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q: Why wouldn't states steal Satoshi's coins?  [VC4]
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ATOMIC ANSWER:
Breaking wallets is Loud warfare.  It reveals QC capability.  This destroys
the attacker's advantage.  Rational actors prioritize Quiet integration.

REFS:
- [BGT-0004] A1.5 (Quantum Defense - Loud vs Quiet strategy)

KEY INSIGHT:
First-mover advantage in quantum belongs to whoever DOESN'T reveal capability.
Using QC to break Bitcoin announces to all adversaries that your defenses
are also vulnerable.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q: Is mining too centralized?  [VC4]
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ATOMIC ANSWER:
Pool concentration is coordinative, not custodial; miners can switch pools
instantly if a pool behaves hostily.

REFS:
- [BGT-0006] A3.3 (Mining Centralization Defense)

KEY INSIGHT:
Pools coordinate block assembly; they don't control hashrate.  The hashrate
itself is distributed across thousands of independent operators who can
redirect within minutes.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q: Why no yield?  [VC1]
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ATOMIC ANSWER:
Yield is taxable income friction; HODL/Debt loops provide tax-neutral
liquidity for long-term wealth preservation.

REFS:
- [BGT-0007] A4.1 (Productive Drag Defense)
- [BGT-0007] A4.2 (Borrow vs Sell Defense)

KEY INSIGHT:
Yield on Bitcoin means counterparty risk + taxable events.
Borrow against Bitcoin = no taxable event + preserve principal.

Q: What if MSTR blows up?  [VC3]
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ATOMIC ANSWER:
MSTR uses low-leverage, long-dated debt; a liquidation is a temporary price
dislocation, not a Bitcoin failure.

REFS:
- [BGT-0006] A3.1 (Large Holder Risk Defense)

KEY INSIGHT:
MSTR's convertible notes (2027-2032) have no margin-call triggers tied to
daily BTC price.  They survived multiple 50%+ drawdowns without liquidation.

EVIDENCE:
- MSTR outperformed major equity benchmarks by 500%+ since 2020 [BGT-0008]
  Entry 2.3.4
- No forced liquidation in any BTC drawdown to date [BGT-0008] Entry 2.3.4

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q: What if ETFs own all the Bitcoin?  [VC3]
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ATOMIC ANSWER:
Owning supply ≠ controlling rules.  Large entity adoption acts as a
"Civilian Shield" that scales defense against physical state capture.

REFS:
- [BGT-0006] A3.2 (ETF Capture Defense)
- [BGT-0005] A2.5 (Scale Deters Attack)

KEY INSIGHT:
ETF custody is ownership, not consensus power.  Confiscating ETF Bitcoin
would require politically impossible actions against millions of voters'
retirement accounts.

AI / AGI QUESTIONS
================================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q: Will AI need Bitcoin?  [Framework]
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ATOMIC ANSWER:
AI can access fiat (bribe for KYC, use proxies).  AI cannot trust fiat—when
betrayed, zero recourse.  Access without trust is worthless.

REFS:
- [BGT-0001] L1-L3 (Limiting Case - AI as zero-recourse actor)
- [BGT-0006] A3.5 (AI Convergence Defense)

KEY INSIGHT:
AI is the strongest proof of Bitcoin's necessity: if it works for entities
with ZERO trust recourse, it works for any trust level.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q: Why does AI prove the thesis?  [Framework]
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ATOMIC ANSWER:
AI is the limiting case: actors with zero trust recourse.  If Bitcoin works
for zero recourse, it works for any recourse level.

REFS:
- [BGT-0001] L2 (Limiting Case proves universal necessity)

KEY INSIGHT:
Humans have courts, police, social pressure.  AI has none.  Whatever money
system works for AI works for everyone—the constraint is strictest.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q: What if AGI fights over resources?  [Framework]
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ATOMIC ANSWER:
Same Exit Game as humans, but betrayal has no consequence.  Only trustless
settlement works when recourse is zero.

REFS:
- [BGT-0006] A3.5 (AI Convergence Defense)

KEY INSIGHT:
AI-to-AI transactions can't rely on reputation, legal systems, or social
norms.  Only cryptographic proof of settlement is credible.

Q: Prove Bitcoin is GTO.  [Framework]
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ATOMIC ANSWER:
All actors face a single Exit Game: Stay in capturable systems or Exit to
neutral settlement.  Exit advantage increases; (Exit, Exit) is the Nash Equilibrium.

REFS:
- [BGT-0001] W1 (World Fork), E1-E4 (Exit Game proofs)
- [BGT-0002] Full formal derivation

KEY INSIGHT:
This is provable from first principles, not historical price.  The game

Q: Why can't they coordinate to ban it?  [VC5]
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ATOMIC ANSWER:
Coalitions require enforcement mechanisms.  Bitcoin's permissionless nature makes
enforcement impossible.  Defectors capture capital; coordination fails at
every level.

REFS:
- [BGT-0001] E2 (Coordination Fails)
- [BGT-0005] A2.1 (Global Ban Defense)

KEY INSIGHT:
Banning Bitcoin is a classic Prisoner's Dilemma.  The first defector captures
all the fleeing capital.  No enforcement mechanism can prevent defection.

GRIDLOCK WEDGE (G1-G6):
The coordination problem is deeper than a simple prisoner's dilemma.
Enforcement actors (CT1-CT3) are themselves in competition with each
other.  Each enforcer preserves the neutral rail not merely to capture
fleeing capital but to hedge against rival enforcers' monetary
weaponization.  Inter-predator competition (ε_jk > 0 in the
Lotka-Volterra model) guarantees the neutral rail survives at
equilibrium ([BGT-0002] Qg3).  No stable suppression coalition
exists because every member gains by defecting ([BGT-0002] Qg6).
See [BGT-0001] §GRIDLOCK WEDGE for the full theorem.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q: Doesn't Bitcoin help criminals?  [VC5]
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ATOMIC ANSWER:
Settlement is not acceptance.  The protocol settles any valid transaction,
but tainted coins carry a provenance discount that disincentivizes the
crime.  Enforcement emerges from rational acceptance decisions, not
protocol censorship.

REFS:
- [BGT-0001] E5 (Settlement vs. Acceptance Note)
- [BGT-PAPER-2] §5.4 (The Acceptance Game)
- [BGT-AUTHOR] §3 (Settlement Is Not Acceptance)

KEY INSIGHT:
Regulated exchanges control fiat on/off ramps and must filter by law.
Tainted coins that cannot convert at par lose economic utility.  Detection
probability increases over time because Bitcoin's ledger is permanent —
chain analysis improves retroactively.  You can steal Bitcoin, but you
cannot easily spend it at full value.  Cash has no such property.

The moral question resolves within the framework: protocol neutrality
(P2, P3) makes enforcement unnecessary at the settlement layer because
rational acceptance decisions at the application layer produce their own
enforcement equilibrium.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q: Does neutral settlement mean everyone must accept Bitcoin?  [VC5]
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ATOMIC ANSWER:
No.  Settlement and acceptance are distinct.  The protocol settles any valid
transaction.  Acceptance is discretionary: exchanges, merchants, and agents
can refuse specific coins based on provenance, sanctions, or policy.

REFS:
- [BGT-0001] E5 (Settlement vs. Acceptance Note)
- [BGT-PAPER-2] §5.4 (Settlement vs. Acceptance)

KEY INSIGHT:
Bitcoin's neutrality applies at the protocol layer — the network processes
any valid transaction without identity or purpose requirements.  But
acceptance is an application-layer decision.  Exchanges can block flagged
UTXOs.  Merchants can decline payment.  AI agents can analyze counterparty
history and refuse to transact.  Neutral settlement provides the
infrastructure; acceptance filtering provides the social layer.  These are
compatible, not contradictory.

Q: Why is everyone buying Bitcoin?  [VC6]
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ATOMIC ANSWER:
Recursive competition: if competitors hedge and it wins, you lose for NOT
hedging.  Pressure cascades at all scales.

REFS:
- [BGT-0001] E4 (Cascades)

The question isn't "will Bitcoin win?" but "can I afford to be wrong if
my competitors are right?" The answer forces allocation regardless of belief.
